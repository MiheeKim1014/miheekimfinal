Seven Forms of

Sustainable Fashion

Click any number

to explore the forms!

1.

On Demand &

Custome Made

There are billions worth unworn clothes languish in people’s wardrobes. Plan your wardrobe carefully and with a long-term view. To begin with, buy a garments that are manufactured on demand or cosrum-made. Impulsive shopping causes unneccessary purchases, so it is important for consumers to buy clothes when they need it, not when they want it. A custome-made clothe will ensure it fits well, which is not often the case for clothes from fast fashion brands. Garments that don’t fit are highly likely to end up being worn only few times or not even once.

2.

Green & Clean

Buy clothes that are made environmentally friendly manner. There are eco-friendly brands who are using renewable and biodegradable materials for their products such as wool, cotton, silk, lyocell, hemp or flax. Avoid non-renewable materials such as polyester, nylon or acrylic, which are derived from petroleum products and cannot typically degrade in nature. Also consider brands who use recyled materials. Over the last few years, there has been a lot of innovation around polyester, a fabric widely used in moisture-wicking clothes.

3.

High Quality &

Timeless Design

Here’s the deal with fast fashion: the quality of the clothing isn’t meant to last and people treat fast fashion like disposable clothing. The clothes end up in landfills. High quality clothes with timeless design will last longer and less likely to be be affected by fashion trends so there is less need to buy new one.

4.

Fair & Ethical

Choosing brands that care their workers. In Colombian mines, Bangladeshi factories, or Vietnam textile mills, labor standards are so low that even though apparel is the largest employer of women globally, less than 2% of these women earn a living wage, according to The Huffington Post. These women can’t even afford to buy the cheap, fast fashion they’re producing to ship overseas.The desire to wear cheap new looks daily has led to offshore manufacturing in often deplorable circumstances – buying local, well-made pieces can sidestep all that.

5.

Repair, Redesign

& Upcycle

Choosing to alter clothes instead of throwing them away is also great for your carbon footprint. It's also a great way to curb excessive spending. By investing in sustainable, good-quality clothes that can be repaired, you're saving the price of the cheap n' nasty garments that fall to pieces after just a few wears.

6.

Rent, Loan & Swap

If you desire something new for more general variation – swap with friends, attend a swapping event, visit a swap-shop or lease clothes from a fashion/apparel store or online rental site. A new trend in the fashion industry is clothing rental. Companies like Rent the Runway and Caastle are helping brands transform their stores into rental services. The idea is that you don’t need to buy fast-fashion items that are in vogue, wear them a few times, then toss them out.

7.

Second Hand

& Vintage

When you need a new clothe, consider to buy a second hand / vintage clothe. Not only is buying secondhand good value for money, it is great for the environment because it ensures that products circulate in the economy longer and stay out of landfills. This helps justify all the labor, raw materials, and energy that went into the manufacturing process. Today, we can find many sustainable brands selling redesigned high quality vintage clothes that is on style and feel brand new.
